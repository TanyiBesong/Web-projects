What is social Media?

Social media is a computer based technology that can be used for the sharing of ideas, thoughts, entertainment, and information through virtual networks and the communities that use them. social media are substantially different from the other media. In contrast to other media, they rather resemble dynamic, interconnected, egalitarian and interactive organisms beyond the control of any organization.


Types of social Media

Social networking sites are the most popular because it allows people to connect with friends, family, and even brands. Some notable social media websites include Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, ect... These sites are all about human to human interaction. The cool thing about most sites like these is you can share thoughts, photos, videos, and even form groups based on similar interests.
Social review sites are for users to give reviews of products, restaurants, hotels, amusement parks, really anything you can give an opinion on.Some examples of social review sites include Yelp and TripAdvisor.
Image sharing sites display images, infographics, and even illustrations that other people can view and react to. Some examples of image sharing sites include Instagram, Snapchat, and VSCO.
These sites are made for users to watch and create videos and post them online for others to see. YouTube is a perfect example of a video hosting site and has grown exponentially since its launch in 2005.
Community blogs are a place for users to convey their thoughts or feelings on any topic online and other users can read and sometimes comment on these articles.
Discussion sites are similar to community blogs but are designed to allow users to discuss certain specific topics. This can be helpful when trying to get information about a subject you don’t know much about, because other users who know more about that subject can give you information without you even knowing them. These are different from most social media because there is less identifiable information for users and most stay anonymous. Examples of discussion sites include Reddit, ASK, and Quora.
Sharing economy networks allow people to sell or rent out things to others. Two examples of sharing economy networks are AirBnB and Ebay.
